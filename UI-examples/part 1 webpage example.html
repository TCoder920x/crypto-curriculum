1. Core Concepts: What is Blockchain?

This section covers the fundamental building blocks of blockchain technology. We'll explore the ideas that make this technology so revolutionary: how it stores data, how participants agree on the truth, and how it can automate processes.

Consensus: PoW vs. PoS

Consensus mechanisms are the rules that allow a decentralized network to agree on which transactions are valid. Proof of Work (PoW) is the original, while Proof of Stake (PoS) is a more energy-efficient evolution.

Smart Contracts Explained

A smart contract is like a digital vending machine. It's a program that runs on the blockchain and automatically executes actions when certain conditions are met, removing the need for intermediaries.

3. The Network in Action

This section explores the mechanics of how the network functions. We'll look at how transactions are processed, the fees involved, and the solutions being developed to make blockchains faster and cheaper for everyone.

Anatomy of a Transaction

Scaling Solutions: L1 vs L2

Layer-2s (L2s) are crucial for making blockchains handle more traffic. They work by processing transactions off the main chain (L1), dramatically increasing speed and reducing fees.

5. The Decentralized Economy

Building on the previous concepts, this section explores the dynamic economy built on blockchain. We'll compare trading venues and dive into the world of Decentralized Finance (DeFi), which aims to rebuild traditional finance without intermediaries.

Trading Venues: CEX vs. DEX

You can trade assets on Centralized Exchanges (CEX) or Decentralized Exchanges (DEX). They offer different models of custody, access, and risk.

Pillars of DeFi

DeFi is composed of various interlocking protocols that let you lend, borrow, swap, and earn yield on your assets, all through smart contracts.
